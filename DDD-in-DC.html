What's Guy Fieri's Problem with DC?

Kate Rabinowitz // September 23, 2015

Guilty pleasure confession: One of my favorite shows is Diner, Drive-Ins and Dives. If you’re uninitiated it’s a show where 90’s emoji Guy Fieri visits restaurants around the country. I can’t be sure what it is exactly- Fieri’s bleached inner goatee, the sunglasses on the back of his head, how he uninvitedly tastes everything in the kitchen or unironically uses phrases like “that’s killer!” and “off the hook!” - but if I’m in a hotel room there’s an 85% chance that show is on.

Guy Fieri Diner,Drive-in and Dives Visits

Source: Restaurants of Diners, Drive-ins and Dives by Maggie Criqui.

The only disappointment is that Guy Fieri has so rarely visited my neck of the woods. Across thirteen seasons Guy Fieri has been to DC only four times. And it’s not like he hasn’t been in the neighborhood. He’s been to fifteen Baltimore restaurants and seven in Virginia Beach. So what’s Guy Fieri’s DC problem?
While, for one, he’s not visiting the best of the DC dining scene. The average Yelp rating for DC restaurants he’s visited is 3.625, lower than most other cities he’s visited and lower than the overall DC restaurant average Yelp rating.
But I think there’s more to it and the lack of Diner, Drive-ins and Dives visits actually reflects a few things about the DC food scene. Much of the show is dedicated to community favorite cheap eats, mom and pop diners, and cultural food institutions. DC doesn't frankly have a high proportion of any of these due to it's more transient population, lack of strong ties to historical immigrant communities and traditionally more of a focus on lawyers who lunch as opposed to casual cheap eats. But I think that's been changing as more people chose to stay longer in the District and a more vibrant food culture takes hold through food trucks, cheap eats like Fast Gourmet, .
On the upside, at least we know for sure Guy Fieri won’t be opening one of his infamous restaurants here anytime soon.
